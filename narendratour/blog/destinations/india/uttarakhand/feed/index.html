Nestled in the breathtaking Garhwal Himalayas, Kartik Swami Temple is a revered spiritual site dedicated to Lord Kartikeya, the elder son of Lord Shiva. This temple, perched on a scenic ridge overlooking deep valleys, offers a unique blend of natural beauty, mythology, and spiritual significance. It attracts devotees and trekkers alike, offering a tranquil escape into the majestic Himalayan landscape.

Location

Kartik Swami Temple is located near Kanakchauri village, along the Rudraprayag-Pokhari route in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, India. The temple sits at an altitude of approximately 3,050 meters (10,007 feet) above sea level and is accessible via a mild trek of about 3 kilometers from Kanakchauri village. The stunning views of the surrounding snow-capped peaks make the journey to the temple a rewarding experience for visitors.

History and Mythology

Kartik Swami Temple is dedicated to Lord Kartikeya, also known as Kartik Murugan Swami in South India. The temple is believed to be the only one of its kind in Uttarakhand dedicated to this deity. According to Hindu mythology, the temple is located at the spot where Lord Kartikeya offered his bones as a testimony of his devotion to Lord Shiva.

The most popular myth associated with Kartikeya is from the Skanda Purana. Once, Lord Shiva challenged his two sons, Kartikeya and Ganesha, to a race around the universe. The winner would earn the right to be worshipped first. Kartikeya set off on his journey around the universe, while Ganesha, in his wisdom, circled around his father, claiming that he was the universe. Pleased with Ganesha’s intelligence, Lord Shiva awarded him the privilege of being worshipped first, leading to Kartikeya’s anger and subsequent sacrifice of his flesh and bones in reverence to his father. It is said that the bones of Lord Kartikeya are worshipped in this temple.

Timing and Entry Fee

Timings

Kartik Swami Temple is open to devotees and visitors throughout the year. The general visiting hours are from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

Entry Fee

As of June 2024, visitors to Kartik Swami Temple will need to pay an entry fee. The fee structure is as follows:

  • Indian Nationals: ₹50
  • Foreign Tourists: ₹100
  • Camera Fee: ₹200
  • Drone Camera Fee: ₹500

Note: Fees are subject to change without prior notice.

Things to Do

1. Trekking

The primary attraction of Kartik Swami Temple is the 3-kilometer trek from Kanakchauri village. This trek offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan ranges and is relatively easy, making it accessible for most visitors. Along the trail, trekkers can enjoy the serene environment and the sounds of nature.

2. Bird Watching

Kartik Swami is a paradise for bird watchers, boasting over 150 species of birds, including the golden eagle, steppe eagle, bearded vulture, Himalayan snowcock, and the vibrant Himalayan monal pheasant.

3. Camping

The area around Kartik Swami is ideal for camping, providing opportunities to connect with nature while enjoying the views of the majestic peaks. Spending a night under the stars in the Himalayas can be a memorable experience.

4. Village Tourism

Visitors can explore Kanakchauri village, which serves as the base camp for the Kartik Swami trek. Here, travelers can experience the local culture, hospitality, and lifestyle of the Garhwal region.

5. Participating in Aarti

Attending the evening Sandhya Aarti (prayers) at the temple is a spiritual experience that attracts many devotees. The enchanting chants and rituals create a serene atmosphere that enhances the connection with the divine.

Places to Visit

1. Ukhimath

Located 50 kilometers away, Ukhimath is an important pilgrimage site and the winter home of Lord Kedarnath. It offers several temples and scenic views of the surrounding mountains.

2. Sari Village

Situated 60 kilometers from Kartik Swami, Sari Village is a picturesque hamlet surrounded by rhododendron and oak trees. It is an excellent spot for nature lovers.

3. Deoriatal

This emerald lake, located 64 kilometers away, is famous for its breathtaking reflections of the Chaukhamba peaks. It’s a popular trekking destination that offers stunning views.

4. Chopta

Known as the “Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand,” Chopta is a charming hamlet located 79 kilometers from Kartik Swami. It is famous for its lush green meadows and offers excellent trekking opportunities.

5. Tungnath

Located 82 kilometers from Kartik Swami, Tungnath is the highest Shiva temple in the world and an essential part of the Panch Kedar pilgrimage. The trek to Tungnath is popular among devotees and trekkers alike.

6. Chandrashila

84 kilometers away, Chandrashila is the summit of the Chandranath Parvat. The trek to the top is rewarding, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas.

How to Reach

By Road

Kartik Swami Temple is well-connected by road, making it accessible from various towns in Uttarakhand. The best way to reach is to take a bus from Haridwar or Rishikesh to Rudraprayag, followed by a shared jeep or taxi to Kanakchauri village.

By Air

The nearest airport to Kartik Swami is Jolly Grant Airport, located 222 kilometers away. From the airport, visitors can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Rudraprayag and then continue to Kanakchauri.

By Train

The nearest railway stations are Rishikesh (205 km) and Haridwar (287 km). From these stations, buses and taxis are available to reach Rudraprayag and then Kanakchauri.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Kartik Swami Temple is from October to June. During this period, the weather is pleasant, and visitors can enjoy clear views of the snow-covered peaks. October and November offer particularly stunning 360-degree views of the Himalayas.

The months from March to May showcase vibrant rhododendron blooms along the trek, while the monsoon (July to September) should be avoided due to heavy rains and landslides.

Accommodation Options

While there are limited accommodation options near the temple, the following places can be considered:

1. Mayadeep Holiday Home

Located in Kanakchauri village, this eco-lodge offers bamboo huts with modern amenities and a closer look at Garhwal village life and culture.

2. Ashram Near the Temple

Managed by local priests, the ashram provides basic accommodation but requires visitors to bring their groceries (rice, flour, tea, etc.) for their stay.

3. Hotels in Rudraprayag

For more comfort, visitors can opt for hotels in Rudraprayag, which is about 40 kilometers away, providing various accommodation options.

Travel Tips

  1. Pack Appropriately: Carry warm clothing, sturdy trekking shoes, and rain gear, as the weather can change rapidly in the mountains.
  2. Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water, especially while trekking, to avoid dehydration.
  3. Carry Snacks: Although there are food options at the temple and nearby, it’s good to carry some light snacks for the trek.
  4. Respect Local Culture: Dress modestly and respect local customs when visiting temples.
  5. Plan Your Trek: Start your trek early in the day to enjoy the views and avoid trekking in the dark.
  6. Check Weather Conditions: Always check weather forecasts before your trip, especially during the monsoon season.

The Chardham Yatra is one of the most revered pilgrimages for Hindus, taking you to four holy destinations in the Indian Himalayas: Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath. Each of these sites has significant spiritual importance and beautiful landscapes that enhance the journey. Here’s a comprehensive guide to help you plan your Chardham Yatra from Mumbai.

Overview of Chardham

Before diving into the planning, let’s understand what each of the four destinations represents:

  • Yamunotri: The source of the Yamuna River, it is dedicated to Goddess Yamuna. The temple here is located amidst stunning mountains, and the trek to reach it is both challenging and rewarding.
  • Gangotri: This is the origin of the Ganges River, revered as the most sacred river in India. The temple is located near the Bhagirathi River, surrounded by breathtaking views.
  • Kedarnath: One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is set against a dramatic backdrop of snow-capped peaks and is accessible only by foot or pony.
  • Badrinath: This temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in India. The beautiful landscape and serene atmosphere add to its spiritual significance.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit the Chardham is between late April and early November. The temples are usually closed during winter due to heavy snowfall. Here’s a quick breakdown of the seasons:

  • April to June: The weather is pleasant, making it ideal for trekking and sightseeing.
  • July to August: Monsoon season can cause landslides; travel is risky.
  • September to November: The weather starts to cool, and it’s less crowded post-monsoon.

Embraced by the purifying waters of the Mandakini River, this temple stands not only as the loftiest Jyotirlinga globally but also serves as the final resting place of the venerable saint, Adi Shankaracharya, who laid the foundation for numerous Mutths and pilgrimage circuits across India.

How to Reach

By Road – Gaurikund serves as the nearest road access point to Kedarnath, marking the starting point for a 14 km trekking expedition. Visitors have the option to drive their private vehicles to Gaurikund and utilize the designated parking area for secure parking.

By Train – Similar to the other Char Dham Yatra destinations, the nearest railway station to Kedarnath is located in Rishikesh. From Rishikesh, you can board a bus or a shared jeep that will take you to Gaurikund.

By Helicopter – The helicopter service to Kedarnath will transport you to one of the helipads situated in Phata, Guptkashi, Sersi, or Sitapur. From there, a convenient chopper service will transfer you to the Kedarnath helipad, located just 500 meters away from the main entrance of the temple.

Best Time to Visit

The optimal time for embarking on the sacred pilgrimage to Kedarnath Dham is during the months of early May to mid-July, coinciding with the opening of the Kapat Dham. It is crucial to plan your visit during this period, especially considering the elevated location of the site.

Badrinath Dham


Dedicated to the divine Badri form of Lord Vishnu, Badrinath Dham, or Badri Vishal, stands out as the most vibrant and colorful temple among the Chota Char Dham sites in Uttarakhand. Nestled in the district of Chamoli, this sacred Dham is said to have risen at the very spot where Lord Vishnu engaged in intense penance, with Goddess Laxmi taking the form of a protective tree. The name “Badrinath” derives from the time when the entire valley was supposedly covered with Badri (jujube) trees, which, unfortunately, are now scarce.

Considered one of the most contemplative and spiritually enlightening temples in India, a visit to Badrinath Dham is deemed essential. The site’s historical significance, coupled with its captivating surroundings, adds to the allure of this sacred destination.

How to Reach

By Road – Interstate buses connect Badrinath with several neighboring cities, including Haridwar, Dehradun, Mussoorie, Gaya Bihar, and Rishikesh. These buses traverse the roads and provide convenient transportation, bringing pilgrims and tourists directly to the entrance of the Badrinath temple. The well-connected road network ensures easy accessibility to this sacred destination from various parts of the surrounding region.

By Train – Rishikesh, serving as the nearest railway station to Badrinath Dham, is well-connected by numerous trains originating from various parts of India. Pilgrims often opt for buses or shared cabs to travel from Rishikesh to Badrinath Dham, ensuring convenient and accessible transportation for their pilgrimage journey.

By Helicopter – You have the option to select from various helicopter service providers that operate flights from Dehradun to the helipad conveniently located near the main temple entrance.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal period for your pilgrimage to this sacred Dham falls within the summer months, spanning from April to mid-July. During this time, the weather is exceptionally delightful, with temperatures ranging from 15 to 25 degrees Celsius, providing a comfortable and convenient travel experience.

Monsoon Weather – The rainy period in this region extends from mid-July to early September, accompanied by a drop in temperature, typically ranging from 8 to 12 degrees Celsius.

Winter Weather – As the winter weather sets in during the early days of October, the entire region becomes blanketed in a thick layer of snow, leading to the closure of darshan and visitation activities.

Flood of 2013:

When Kedarnath Dham succumbed to the surging waters from Chorabari Tal, triggered by an abrupt cloudburst, the entire region underwent a thorough cleansing. The deluge obliterated everything in its path, sparing only the main temple structure, sheltered by a massive mountain rock. Tragically, numerous pilgrims lost their lives as the floodwaters engulfed the surroundings. In the aftermath, Kedarnath Dham embarked on a journey of reconstruction.

The trekking route, once ravaged by the calamitous flood, has now been reestablished along a new and safer path. Although some shops and local tea stalls have been reconstructed, the reconstruction efforts have led to a somewhat congested layout. The pathways leading to the main entrance have been expanded for improved accessibility.

Diligently, various expert agencies and construction teams collaborated on the redevelopment project of Kedarnath Dham, ensuring that the sacred site regains its former glory while incorporating enhanced safety measures.

Char Dham Railway

The Char Dham Railway stands as a proposed initiative by the Indian Railways, featuring a dual rail line system designed to complement the ongoing highway project connecting all the pilgrimage sites of Chota Char Dham Yatra. The railway journey for Char Dham Yatra is set to commence from the Doiwala railway station near Dehradun. The route will extend towards Gangotri and Yamunotri through a split at Uttarkashi.

Simultaneously, a distinct railway line will connect the forthcoming railway station in Karnaprayag, branching off towards Kedarnath and Badrinath via the Saikot junction. This comprehensive rail network aims to enhance accessibility and convenience for pilgrims undertaking the sacred Char Dham Yatra, offering an alternative and efficient mode of transportation alongside the existing highway project.

Char Dham All-Weather Road Project

The Char Dham Mahamarg Vikas Pariyojna is an ambitious highway development initiative undertaken by the central government for the state of Uttarakhand. This visionary project focuses on the comprehensive enhancement and modernization of the road network that connects the sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites, namely Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri.

With an estimated budget of nearly 12,000 crore rupees, this monumental undertaking aims to cover an extensive distance of 1,100 kilometers. The primary objective is to address the existing challenges faced by the Char Dham roads, which are currently ill-equipped to withstand all weather conditions. Some of these roads are susceptible to dust and mud, hindering the pilgrimage experience for devotees.

Char Dham Yatra FAQs

Q1. Is It Permissible To Bring My Infant Child To Char Dham?

Ans. Parents are discouraged from traveling with children under the age of 2 due to unfavorable weather conditions and the overall environment, which may have adverse effects on the well-being of the kids.

Q2. What Are The Commencement And Conclusion Dates Of The Char Dham Yatra?

Ans. The Char Dham Yatra begins in the early summer months of April or May and continues until the onset of winter, typically concluding by the end of October or early November. You have the flexibility to embark on the Char Dham Yatra at a time that suits your comfort and convenience.

Q3. Is Embarking On The Char Dham Yatra Journey A Secure Choice For Individuals Aged 70 And Above?

Ans. With the evolution of diverse transportation services and the introduction of additional amenities, the Char Dham Yatra has become easily accessible for individuals who may be elderly or physically incapable of undertaking the journey. Opting for a helicopter Yatra is now the most convenient choice, significantly reducing the duration of your pilgrimage while offering the utmost comfort and convenience in reaching Char Dham.

Q4. Is It Possible For Me To Travel From Kolkata To Kedarnath?

Ans. Certainly! It is indeed possible to visit Kedarnath Dham from Kolkata. To begin your journey, you will need to travel to Haridwar or Rishikesh via New Gaya Bihar. From there, you can choose to travel to Kedarnath Dham by bus, taxi, jeep, or private car. The final stretch of the journey, up to Gaurikund, is accessible by motorable road.
