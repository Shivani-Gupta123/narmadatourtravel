]]> Popularly known as the ‘queen of hill stations’ and located, at a distance of 86 kms from Coimbatore, Ooty is a refreshing hamlet, for people, wanting to get away, from the hustle and bustle of the busy city life. Offering lush green glimpses of the surrounding beauty and replete with unparalleled raw natural beauty Ooty has always been a center for tourist activities. The plunging green valleys, clean environment and the unpolluted air are what makes Ooty one of the most lovable destinations for all sorts of travellers.

Experience the Beauty of Mother Nature – Visit Ooty

Ooty the “Queen of hill stations” is also known as Udhagamandalam or Udhagai. During the Colonial time it was the favorite place for the British to spend their summer and weekend holidays. The supercilious mountains, impenetrable forest, extensive grasslands and miles and miles of tea gardens welcome the tourists. The main attraction of Ooty is its annual Tea and Tourism Festival. Located in the Nilgiri Ranges, Ooty is the perfect location for honeymoon or family vacations. So for your Honeymoon opt for Ooty honeymoon package or for your family vacation opt for Ooty packages.

Get lost in the beautiful valley of Ooty

In your Ooty tourism packages you must have to include Government Rose Garden, Ooty Lake, Ooty Botanical Gardens, Toda huts, Stone House, Ooty Mountain Railway, St. Stephen’s Church, Ooty Golf Course, Wax World, and Tribal Museum. There are innumerable tourist spots around Ooty which you must add in your Ooty tour like Doddabetta Peak, Pykara, Wenlock Downs, Pine forest, Mudumalai National Park, Upper Bhavani, Emerald and Avalanche Lake and many more. In Ooty you must go for bird watching, boating in Ooty Lake and Pykara Lake, experiencing sunset in Doddabetta peak, trekking, and hiking in Nilgiri Mountain range.

Places to See in Ooty

Doddabetta Peak

The highest peak in the Hills of the Nilgiris (2,623 metres), this place falls right at the crossroads of the Eastern and the Western Ghats. This place should definitely be included on your visit to Ooty since this will be a refreshing experience. Situated at a mere distance of almost 10 kms from the main town of Ooty, this place offers breathtaking views of the panoramic surrounding. There is also a Telescope House which is maintained by the Tamil Nadu Tourism Development Corporation and also enables the tourists to get a better view.

Government Botanical Gardens

Ooty botanical garden attracts tourists from all over the world. Flourishing, green, well-maintained lawns, rare species of trees, a 20 million-year-old fossil tree, an Italian – style garden bordering, a variety of flowers, a range of ferns and orchids along with the presence of a crystal clear pool are some of the attraction points for this garden. The “Summer Festival” held each year at the Botanical Garden during the month of May is again popular. The pride of the festival still remains a colorful and vibrant slower show.

Pykara Waterfalls

The Pykara River is famed for being the largest river in the Nilgiris District. It gushes down majestically in a series of cascades and the last two gushes down the remaining 55 meters and 61 meters forming the Pykara falls. The boating here is a lot of fun, so do try it if you are in Ooty. Wenlock Downs is a vast expanse of grassy meadows on way to Pykara, is very popular with the movie makers in the country.

Rose Park

Rose Park is situated in the heart of Ooty less than a km from Charring Cross at Vizianagaram. The garden has the largest collections of roses in the country (around 3000 varieties) which includes Hybrid Tea Roses, miniature rose, Floribunda, Ramblers, etc. It has also received the certificate of being a “Garden of Excellence” by the World Federation of Rose Societies (WFRS) in 2006.

St. Stephen’s Church

The immaculate St Stephen’s Church, was constructed in 1829, and is the oldest church in the Nilgiris, with lovely stained glass.

Kalhatty Waterfalls

Located on the slopes of Kalhatty, these cascading falls are about 100 feet high and located at a distance of about 13kms from Ooty. There are several wild species like panthers, Sambhar, bison and wild buffaloes endemic to this region.

Avalanche Lake

Avalanche Dam and Lake is located at a distance of 25 km from Ooty. Trekking is quite popular here. The nearby trout farm is the only place in India where trout fish grow. The lake is ideal for fishing and bird watching.

Tourist Places near Ooty

Mudumalai National Park

Located at a distance of about 67kms from the wonderful hilly hamlet of Ooty, this Wildlife sanctuary is rich and filled with a varied collection of flora and fauna. It is also home to innumerable rare species of birds and animals. You can also easily get a glimpse of animals like elephants, giant squirrels, Sambhar, chital, barking deer and flying squirrels and birds including colorful parakeets, black woodpecker, Malabar trogon and crested hawk eagle.

Coonoor

Located at a comfortable distance of 17 kms from Ooty, the famous hill station of Coonoor stands at an elevation of 1839 meters above sea level. In upper Coonoor lies Sim’s Park, which houses more than 1000 plant species, including many flowering plants, pines, ferns and shrubs.

How to Reach Ooty

Air: The Coimbatore airport is the closest to Ooty. From Coimbatore, it takes 3.1/2 hours by road to reach Ooty. There are flights into Coimbatore from Singapore, Sharjah, Colombo and most parts of India including Mumbai (Kingfisher, Indian Airlines, Jet and Go Air), Chennai (Kingfisher, Indian Airlines and Jet Airways) and Bangalore.

Road: Ooty is well connected with a network of top-notch roads to almost all the south Indian cities. Regular bus services are available which runs from Mysore, Bangalore, Madurai, and Kanyakumari and also from many cities in Kerala. Private and state-owned vehicles run regularly on this route. The Coimbatore-Ooty road is the most well maintained and frequently used road.

Rail: Mettupalayam, is located at a distance of 47 km from Ooty, is the nearest major railway station. There are regular train services available from Coimbatore and Chennai to Mettupalayam. Mettupalayam and Coonoor are connected to Ooty with toy trains and the route is very picturesque. Regular Taxi services are available from Mettupalayam to Ooty.

When to Visit Ooty

The weather of Ooty is generally pleasant all year round and it can be visited any time of the year. The most suitable period to visit the hill station are the months of February, March, October and November. While summers are charming and colorful, the winters in Ooty are relatively colder compared to other parts of South India. During the summers the temperature ranges from 10 to 25 degrees, while during the winters it ranges between 5 and 21 degrees centigrade.

]]> Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, Ootacamund, and Udhagai, is a popular hill station and the district capital of the Nilgiris district in the Tamil Nadu state of India. Located in the Nilgiri hills, it is at the height of 7,500 feet above the sea level. Officially, it is named as Udhagamandalam.

It is not clear as to how the name Ootacamund was derived, however ‘mund’ refers to the Badaga word for a Toda village. Further, Ootaca is derived from the local Tamil language, Otta-Cal, meaning single stone. This is actually referring a holy stone that is worshipped by the native Toda tribe.

Windy hilly roads, complex rack railway system, lofty mountains, calm lakes, thick forests, slouching pampas, enormous tea gardens, and high eucalyptus trees welcome the visitors to Ooty.

History
In the medieval period, the Nilgiris was ruled by the Chera Empire. Then, it was under the Ganga dynasty succeeded by the Hoysala Empire whose king was Vishnuvardhana in the 12th century. Later, it fell into the hands of the Kingdom of Mysore of Tipu Sultan and was then under the rule of the British in the 18th century.

The British governor of Coimbatore, John Sullivan, was pleased with this town’s climate. Therefore, he bought the land from the local inhabitants (Kota/Kotas, Toda, Kurumba, and Badaga); at a cost of few feasts.

Exclusively owned by the private British citizens, this area flourished more quickly during the British rule as compared to the rest of India. It was the summer capital of the Madras region and the weekend gateway during the colonial era. Due to its spectacular scenes and evergreen valleys, British entitled the place as ‘Queen of Hills’.

Tourists’ Attractions

Ooty is a part of the Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve to safeguard the frail ecosystem. Due to this, many of the forest areas and water lakes are not open to visitors. This is because unplanned tourism this has resulted in haywire commercialization that has negatively affected the ecosystem in Ooty.

Ooty Lake and Boat House

Built by John Sullivan, in olden days, it was quite large such that it covered the current bus stand, race course and maximum portion of the present market. Boating attracts the tourists that allow them to have the benefit of calm environs look amidst the groves of eucalyptus trees boosting the scenic beauty. You can also enjoy the nearby Mini Garden that has an amusement park and a toy train for children.

Stone House

Erected by John Sullivan, this was the first ‘Pukka’ house, now in old Ooty. Located within the premises of the Government Arts College, it is the government office currently.

Toda Huts
These are an attractive residing place of Todas who still live up in the hills of the Botanical Garden.

Fernhills Palace

This is a must visiting palace in Ooty.

Railway Station

Being a part of World Heritage Site and the station of the Nilgiri Mountain Railway, it offers a unique view of the rail track built during the British rule.

St. Stephen’s Church

Being one of the oldest churches in Ooty, its sculpting reveals its ancient style along with the tall spires. This must be visiting church is near the District Courthouse building.

Wax World
This is a museum that exhibits wax statues of the Indian history personalities almost seem to look alive. It also houses instances of Indian culture and heritage in a 142-year-old bungalow.

Ooty Golf Links
This is a grassy forest area for playing golf.

Doddabetta Peak

This is the highest point in the Nilgiri Hills and is surrounded by the reserved forest area. You can enjoy the amazing views of Ooty from here. You can reach this peak through a hilly road.

Tea Plantations
Due to their scenic nature, tourists always visit these lands of cultivation at a lower altitude.

Ketti Valley
Due to ever-pleasant climate, this is also known as the ‘Switzerland of Southern India’.

Valley View
This is a point that lies in between the Ooty- road.

Pykara Lake Boat House and Pykara Falls

This is the famous picnic spot. However, most of the part of the lake is not opened to the visitors.

Pine Forest
Comprising of systematically arranged pines, it is a small region at the base of the hill between Ooty and Thalaikundha. This tourist spot was also shown in the Tamil movie song ‘Deena’.

Wenlock Downs
Being a grassland area, it comprises of rolling hills and is compared with the regions of the British Isles like the Yorkshire Dales. This region is mostly a film shooting area, especially the two areas at six and nine miles (14 km) on the Ooty-Pykara road, which is also known as the Mysore road. Based on this distance, the two areas are named as the Sixth Mile and Ninth Mile respectively.

Kamraj Sagar Lake

This is located in between the route to Pykara Lake.

Mudumalai National Park

Housing variety of flora and fauna, it is at a lower altitude and shares its border with the Bandipur National Park in Karnataka.

Mukurthi National Park
Housing a peak and a lake of this name too, this is a protected area not opened for visitors.

Needle Hill View Point

This is a point located in between Gudalur and Pykara.

Parsons Valley Reservoir
This is the chief source of water for the Ooty people and forms a part of the reserved forest area.

Emerald Lake

Located near the Emerald town, it houses a dam and a viewpoint.

Avalanche Lake

This is near to the Emerald Lake and is a part of the reserved forest area.

Porthimund Lake

This lake is a part of the reserved forest lake and hence, is inaccessible.

Upper Bhavani Lake

This lake is a part of the Mukurthi National Park and hence, is inaccessible.

Hang Gliding Sport at Kalahatty

20 km from Ooty, Kalahatty in the Nilgiri ranges is famous for hang gliding. This is an adventure sport wherein you hang from a large kite controlled known as the hang glider. Training courses are conducted from March to May.

How to Reach
By Road: Ooty is well connected with the states of Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Karnataka through the five main Nilgiri Ghat Roads. Frequent buses are available from the nearby towns – Coonoor, Kotagiri, and Gudalur. Bus services are also present in different cities of Tami Nadu.

By Rail: Ooty is linked with Chennai (formerly Madras) by a night train service. Mettupalayam, at the foot of Nilgiris, is the junction between ‘The Nilagiri Passenger’ (meter gauge) and the Nilgiri Express (broad gauge).

By Air: The nearest airport is Coimbatore and Calicut (146 km).
