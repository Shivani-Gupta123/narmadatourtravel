]]> If you want to have the best memories during your Kailash Mansarovar Yatra then planning is important. Planning a trip to Tibetan regions such as Kailash which are highly protected can be tricky without the correct information. From physical training to packing essentials and obtaining the required permits, there are several important steps that you need to undertake for a seamless pilgrimage. You can apply for the yatra with a private tour operator or the government of India. We have curated a guide to help you prepare for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra in the best way.

Choose the Best Route

You need to decide beforehand whether you want to go with a private tour operator or the Government of India.

Kailash Yatra with Govt. Of India: Kailash Mansarovar Yatra is organized by the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) of the Government of India through two different routes. You need to choose the route you want to undertake for the yatra.

The first route for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra is in Uttarakhand via Lipulekh Pass. A total of 18 groups can complete the yatra through this route with a maximum of 60 pilgrims in each group. Earlier it cost approx 1.8 L and used to take around 24 days. Now a new road has been built to Mt Kailash, reducing the duration of the yatra to 19-20. The cost is now higher than 2 L.

The second route is Kailash Yatra by Govt of India in Sikkim through the Nathu La Pass. A total of 350 pilgrims can complete the yatra by opting for this route. By opting for this route the yatra can be completed in 21 days. Before COVID-19, the Yatra cost 2.5 lakhs. However, when the yatra started again, costing went much higher.

Best Route & Private Agency for Kailash Yatra

You can also choose a reputed private tour operator for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra. This way, you can get the option to go over land or by helicopter. If you choose a private agency, the yatra can take between 5 and 14 days.

  • The first one is the helicopter route via Nepal. The duration of this yatra is between 5 to 11 days. The best thing about this Kailash Mansarovar Yatra Route is that its starting point is India.
  • Another route you can select is Kailash Mansarovar Yatra by Road. This route starts from Kathmandu and its duration is around 14 days.

Both Nepal routes are more pocket-friendly and take less time in comparison to the Kailash Yatra organized by Govt. Of India. Because of this most pilgrims choose Nepal Route for their Mansarovar Kailash Yatra.

Procedure to Apply for Kailash Mansarovar Yatra with the Government Of India?

You can apply for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra online. Take a look at the steps you need to follow to register for the yatra.

  • Visit MEA Website: Firstly you need to visit the website http://kmy.gov.in to apply for the Yatra. You will have to choose between two routes that seem to be the most suitable for you. This will help the officials in knowing your preference when the lots are drawn.
  • Total Pilgrims allowed for KMY with MEA: 18 batches of 60 pilgrims each are allowed for the Nathu La Pass route. They will be allowed to go annually. 7 batches of 50 pilgrims will be allowed to go annually through the Lipulekh pass route.
  • Kailash Mansarovar Yatra Most Affordable Rate: If you choose to go for the Yatra via Nathula Pass in Sikkim, you will have to pay around Rs 2.5 lakh while pilgrims who choose the Lipulekh Pass route in Uttarakhand, will have to pay around Rs 1.8 lakh. After making the selection you will have to pay the money.
  • Requirements for Kailash Mansarovar Yatra with Govt. of India: There are a few essential things you will need to apply. You will have to send your scanned recent passport photo, your email address, scanned passport, and your phone number. Check whether your passport is valid as there should be at least six months left at the start of the journey day.
  • Medical fitness requirement for Kailash Mansarovar Yatra with Govt. of India: Before embarking on the yatra you will be tested medically in Gaya Bihar. An experienced doctor will check you physically and if you do not meet the required parameters for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra, you will not be allowed to undertake the yatra. Thus, you must have an active lifestyle and you should be physically fit if you decide to undertake the Mt. Kailash yatra. To ensure you clear all the important medical examinations, you should eat well-balanced meals, start an exercise regimen, and take your medicines on time.
  • Documentation required for Kailash Yatra with the Ministry of External Affairs: If you are selected for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra, you will be required to submit important documents like an indemnity bond that clearly specifies that you are responsible for any risk that you encounter during the journey. You will also have to produce an undertaking for helicopter evacuation if there is an emergency. A consent form also needs to be signed declaring that if you die the cremation of your mortal remains will be done on the Chinese side.
  • Regarding incorrect details for KMY: Don’t provide any incorrect details on your application form to the GOI. Your application will be rejected if you’re found guilty of submitting false information.

Procedure to Apply for Kailash Mansarovar Yatra with a Private Tour Operator

If you choose a private tour operator for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra you will have to follow the following steps for registration.

  • Download the Application Form in PDF Format for Kailash Yatra: You will have to visit the private agency’s website that you have selected. Download the Kailash Yatra’s PDF application form. Carefully read the form.
  • Fill up the Yatra Form: Provide the right information in the form else it will be rejected. Whether it is errors regarding age or spelling mistakes, if you provide wrong information your application will be rejected.
  • Send All the Important Documents: You will need to submit all the important documents required for the yatra to your private tour operator. You will have to send the documents scanned copies by email.
  • Other Documents Required for Mansarovar Kailash Yatra: Attach the first and last page of the passport copy, PAN card copy and pay registration amount, and 3 passport-size photographs. You must submit a physical fitness certificate from your practitioner, your original passport, and the rest of the payment at least a month before the Kailash Yatra date.

If you decide to go with a private tour operator for the Kailash Yatra you should choose a trustworthy agency with high credentials. This will ensure that your journey is hassle-free.

]]> Nestled deep within the Tibetan Plateau in the Trans-Himalayan range, Mount Kailash stands as a symbol of divine power and eternal sanctity. This majestic, snow-capped peak, considered the earthly home of Lord Shiva, has been revered for millennia by Hindus, Buddhists, Jains, and followers of the Bon tradition. While the pilgrimage to Mount Kailash and Lake Manasarovar traditionally involves an arduous journey over treacherous terrain, the Kailash Mansarovar Aerial Darshan offers a transformative experience, combining modern convenience with profound spiritual significance. In this guide, we explore the nuances of this unique pilgrimage, the significance of Mount Kailash, and everything you need to know about the aerial darshan.

Spiritual Significance of Mount Kailash

Mount Kailash holds a mystical allure that draws devotees from across the world. Its spiritual importance transcends geographical boundaries and religious distinctions, making it a universally revered destination.

  1. Hinduism
    In Hindu mythology, Mount Kailash is regarded as the abode of Lord Shiva, the god of destruction and regeneration. The mountain is considered a natural manifestation of the Shiva Lingam, a symbol of cosmic creation. It is believed that circumambulating the mountain (known as the Kailash Parikrama) washes away a devotee’s sins and liberates them from the cycle of birth and rebirth (samsara). Additionally, Lake Manasarovar, located near the base of Mount Kailash, is said to have been created by Lord Brahma and is considered a source of spiritual purification. Devotees believe that a dip in its holy waters can cleanse the soul and provide divine insight.
  2. Buddhism
    For Tibetan Buddhists, Mount Kailash is the spiritual home of Demchok, the Buddha who embodies supreme bliss. It is also the center of a cosmological mandala, representing the axis mundi (the spiritual axis of the universe). Buddhists also perform the parikrama (known as kora) around Mount Kailash, believing it to be an act of immense merit that accelerates the path to enlightenment.
  3. Jainism
    In Jain cosmology, Mount Kailash is known as Ashtapada, the site where the first Jain Tirthankara, Rishabhadeva, attained nirvana. For Jains, a pilgrimage to Mount Kailash is a tribute to this spiritual leader and a means to deepen their spiritual practice.
  4. Bon Religion
    For followers of the ancient Bon religion of Tibet, Mount Kailash is a sacred site where their founder, Tonpa Shenrab, descended from the heavens. It is considered a place of great power, and the Bon people circumambulate the mountain in a counterclockwise direction, unlike the clockwise practice of Hindus and Buddhists.

The Advent of the Kailash Aerial Yatra

The Kailash Mansarovar Yatra has traditionally been a physically demanding journey, involving weeks of travel through high-altitude terrain, often in harsh weather conditions. However, the introduction of the Kailash Aerial Yatra has revolutionized this pilgrimage, making it more accessible and convenient for a wider range of devotees. The aerial darshan allows pilgrims to experience the divine energy of Mount Kailash and Lake Manasarovar from the sky, offering a unique perspective on these sacred sites.

  1. Aerial Yatra Benefits
    • Accessibility: For many devotees, especially the elderly, those with physical disabilities, or individuals with medical conditions that make high-altitude trekking dangerous, the traditional yatra is nearly impossible. The aerial darshan offers a safe and convenient alternative, making the pilgrimage accessible to more people.
    • Time-Efficiency: The overland yatra can take several weeks, but the aerial darshan can be completed in a matter of hours. This is particularly beneficial for those who cannot take extended time off from work or family obligations.
    • Comfort: The aerial yatra eliminates the physical strain of trekking through rugged terrain and enduring extreme weather conditions. Pilgrims can enjoy a comfortable and serene journey without compromising their spiritual goals.
    • Bird’s-Eye View: The aerial route offers a breathtaking, panoramic view of Mount Kailash and Lake Manasarovar, providing pilgrims with a perspective that cannot be attained on the ground.
    • Safety: High-altitude sickness is a serious concern for many pilgrims attempting the traditional route. The aerial darshan minimizes the risk of altitude-related health issues, offering a safer option for those unaccustomed to such extreme environments.
    • Year-Round Operation: While the overland yatra is limited to specific months due to weather conditions, the aerial darshan can be conducted throughout the year, subject to local laws and weather patterns.

Planning Your Kailash Mansarovar Aerial Darshan

Before embarking on this spiritually enriching journey, it’s important to plan your aerial darshan meticulously. From selecting the right tour operator to understanding the itinerary and ensuring that you have the required permits, here’s a step-by-step guide to booking your Kailash Aerial Yatra.

  1. Selecting a Tour Operator
    There are several tour operators that offer Kailash Aerial Yatra packages. When choosing an operator, consider the following:

    • Reputation: Look for operators with a proven track record of organizing spiritual tours, particularly to Kailash Mansarovar.
    • Licensing and Permits: Ensure the operator has the necessary permits and licenses to conduct aerial darshan tours in the region.
    • Customer Reviews: Read testimonials and reviews from previous clients to gauge the quality of service.
    • Safety Protocols: Confirm that the operator adheres to all necessary safety protocols, including the provision of trained pilots and properly maintained aircraft.
    • Price Transparency: Be clear about the total cost of the tour, including any hidden fees or additional charges for permits, meals, or accommodations.
  2. Understanding the Itinerary
    The typical aerial yatra itinerary includes a flight over Mount Kailash and Lake Manasarovar, along with views of surrounding Himalayan peaks. Depending on the tour operator, the itinerary may also include visits to nearby temples or other spiritually significant locations. Review the itinerary carefully to ensure it aligns with your expectations.
  3. Permit and Documentation
    Since Mount Kailash is located in Tibet, a region under Chinese control, international travelers will require a Tibet Travel Permit in addition to a Chinese visa. Tour operators typically assist with obtaining these permits, but it’s important to ensure you have all the necessary documents well in advance of your trip. Additionally, some operators may require medical clearance, especially if there are concerns about altitude or air travel.
  4. Booking and Payment
    Once you’ve selected your tour operator and itinerary, you’ll need to book your trip and make an initial deposit. It’s advisable to book early, as the Kailash Aerial Yatra is popular, and slots can fill up quickly. Be sure to clarify the payment schedule and any cancellation or refund policies before making a payment.
  5. Packing Essentials
    Although the aerial darshan is much more comfortable than the traditional yatra, it’s still important to pack appropriately:

    • Warm Clothing: Even in the aircraft, temperatures can drop significantly at high altitudes, so dress in layers and bring a warm jacket.
    • Travel Documents: Ensure you have your passport, visa, permits, and other required documents easily accessible.
    • Camera/Phone: The views from the aircraft are spectacular, so bring a camera or smartphone to capture the experience. However, be mindful of any restrictions on photography in certain areas.
    • Spiritual Items: Many pilgrims bring religious items such as prayer beads or images of deities to enhance their spiritual experience during the flight.
  6. Pre-Tour Briefing
    Most tour operators conduct a pre-tour briefing to provide essential information about the flight, safety protocols, and what to expect during the aerial darshan. It’s important to attend this briefing to ensure a smooth and safe experience.

The Cost of Kailash Aerial Darshan

The cost of the Kailash Aerial Yatra can vary depending on several factors, including the duration of the tour, the services provided, and the tour operator. A typical aerial yatra package may range from ₹50,000 to ₹70,000 per person. This cost usually includes:

  • Transportation: Pickup and drop-off from a designated location (e.g., Kathmandu, Nepalgunj).
  • Aerial Darshan: The helicopter flight over Mount Kailash and Lake Manasarovar.
  • Meals: Meals during the duration of the tour.
  • Accommodation: If the package includes an overnight stay, accommodation at a comfortable hotel or guesthouse is typically included.

It’s important to clarify what is included in the package price and whether there are any additional charges for permits, travel insurance, or other services.

The Kailash Mansarovar Aerial Darshan offers a unique opportunity to experience one of the most sacred sites on earth without the physical demands of the traditional pilgrimage. ]]> The Kailash Mansarovar Yatra is a pilgrimage of profound spiritual significance for many followers of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Bon. Mount Kailash, considered the sacred abode of Lord Shiva, and the nearby Lake Mansarovar hold a revered place in these religions. Undertaking this journey is a significant endeavor due to the challenging mountainous terrain and unpredictable weather conditions. However, for those who seek a more convenient way to experience this pilgrimage, the helicopter route offers an efficient and less physically demanding alternative.

Advantages of Choosing the Helicopter Route

  1. Time Efficiency: The helicopter route significantly reduces travel time compared to traditional trekking routes. While the traditional routes can take up to three weeks, the helicopter route typically takes between 5 to 11 days. This makes it an ideal choice for individuals with limited time but a deep desire to undertake this sacred journey.
  2. Cost-Effectiveness: Opting for the helicopter route can be more economical. For instance, the helicopter tour offered by NamasteIndiaTrip is priced around Rs 1,85,000, which is often less expensive than the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) organized trips. This cost-effectiveness makes the yatra more accessible to those on a budget.
  3. Simplified Medical Requirements: The helicopter route involves less physical exertion than the traditional trekking routes, leading to more lenient medical evaluations. This is particularly beneficial for those who may find the rigorous health checks of the MEA routes challenging.
  4. Reduced Risk: The helicopter journey involves minimal trekking and significantly lowers the risks associated with high-altitude travel. Most of the journey is conducted via air travel, thus reducing the physical strain on participants.

Detailed Itinerary of the Helicopter Route

Day 1: Arrival in Kathmandu

Upon arrival in Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, you will be transferred to your hotel. This day is dedicated to resting and acclimatizing to the local environment. After checking in and freshening up, you will have the opportunity to explore Kathmandu’s main attractions, including its vibrant markets and historic temples. You will spend the night at your hotel in Kathmandu, preparing for the next leg of your journey.

Day 2: Travel to Nepalgunj

The following morning, you will embark on a flight from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj. Nepalgunj is a key transit point for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra. Upon arrival, you will be transferred to your hotel where you will have the chance to rest. A briefing session will be conducted to inform you about the upcoming journey. This meeting provides an opportunity to address any questions or concerns you may have. After a relaxing evening and dinner, you will spend the night at the hotel in Nepalgunj.

Day 3: Journey to Simikot and Hilsa

On the third day, you will take a flight from Nepalgunj to Simikot. Simikot is a small town in the Humla District and serves as a transit point before heading to the Tibetan border. After a short rest and refreshment break in Simikot, you will board a helicopter to Hilsa. Hilsa is located near the border of Tibet and is a crucial point for crossing into the Tibetan region. After arriving in Hilsa, you will drive to Purang, where you will check into a hotel for an overnight stay.

Day 4: Acclimatization in Purang

The next day is dedicated to acclimatization in Purang. Given the high altitude, acclimatization is crucial to adjust to the reduced oxygen levels. Short acclimatization treks may be conducted to help you adapt. This day allows you to prepare for the higher altitudes you will encounter later in the journey. You will spend the night at a hotel in Purang.

Day 5: Visit to Lake Mansarovar

The fifth day involves a drive from Purang to Lake Mansarovar in an air-conditioned Volvo bus. The drive takes approximately 2 hours and offers scenic views of the Tibetan landscape. Upon reaching Lake Mansarovar, you will be able to perform the ‘parikrama’ (circumambulation) of the lake, a significant ritual in the pilgrimage. The lake, considered sacred, is also a place where many pilgrims take a holy dip. This day is spent near the lake, and you will stay overnight at accommodations near Lake Mansarovar.

Day 6: Drive to Darchen

From Lake Mansarovar, you will drive to Darchen, a small town at the base of Mount Kailash. Darchen serves as the starting point for the ‘parikrama’ (circumambulation) of Mount Kailash. The journey from Lake Mansarovar to Darchen is relatively short, allowing you to rest and prepare for the trek around the sacred mountain. Overnight stay is at a hotel or guesthouse in Darchen.

Days 7-9: ‘Parikrama’ of Mount Kailash

The ‘parikrama’ of Mount Kailash, a key component of the pilgrimage, typically spans three days. This circumambulation of the mountain is performed in a clockwise direction, which is considered spiritually auspicious. The trek is physically demanding but rewarding, offering profound spiritual experiences. Pilgrims often find this part of the journey both challenging and fulfilling. The trek will lead you back to Darchen, completing the parikrama.

Days 10-11: Return Journey to Kathmandu

After completing the ‘parikrama,’ you will retrace your steps back to Kathmandu. The return journey follows the same route used for arrival, involving a helicopter ride from Darchen to Hilsa, followed by a flight to Simikot, a flight to Nepalgunj, and finally, a flight back to Kathmandu. From Kathmandu, you will catch your flight back home, concluding your sacred journey.

Preparing for the Yatra

Health Considerations: Despite the helicopter route being less physically taxing than trekking routes, altitude acclimatization is crucial. Ensure you are in good health and consult with a healthcare provider before undertaking the journey.

Documentation: Ensure all necessary permits and documentation are in order before departure. This includes travel permits for Tibet and any specific requirements for the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra.

Travel Insurance: Consider purchasing comprehensive travel insurance that covers high-altitude travel and any potential medical emergencies.

Packing Essentials: Pack appropriately for high-altitude travel. This includes warm clothing, sturdy walking shoes, and essential medications.

Local Customs and Etiquette: Familiarize yourself with local customs and practices to show respect during your pilgrimage.

Conclusion

The Kailash Mansarovar Yatra by helicopter offers a unique opportunity to experience one of the most sacred pilgrimages in a more accessible and less physically demanding manner. With the benefits of reduced travel time, cost-effectiveness, and simplified medical requirements, this route provides a viable option for those eager to undertake this spiritual journey. By following the outlined itinerary and preparing adequately, you can ensure a fulfilling and transformative experience on this sacred pilgrimage.

Significance of Kailash Mansarovar Yatra

The Kailash Mansarovar Yatra holds an unparalleled significance in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Bon religions, revered as the abode of divine energy and spiritual enlightenment. Mount Kailash, towering majestically in the heart of the Himalayas, is considered the throne of Lord Shiva, the ultimate ascetic and source of creation and destruction. Lake Mansarovar, nestled at the base of Mount Kailash, is believed to cleanse the soul of sins and grant spiritual purification. Undertaking this pilgrimage is not merely a physical journey but a profound quest for inner transformation and divine blessings.

Highlights of Mansarovar Yatra

  • Mythological Significance: Mount Kailash stands as a symbol of cosmic energy and spiritual power, attracting pilgrims from far and wide to pay homage to the divine presence of Lord Shiva.
  • Visit to Lake Mansarovar: The pristine waters of Lake Mansarovar, reflecting the towering peaks of the Himalayas, evoke a sense of serenity and awe, inviting pilgrims to immerse themselves in its purifying embrace.
  • Sacred Walk of Kailash: Embarking on the circumambulation (kora) of Mount Kailash is a journey of self-discovery and spiritual renewal, where every step taken is a testament to one’s devotion and resilience.

How difficult is Kailash Mansarovar Yatra

The Kailash Mansarovar Yatra is not merely a physical journey but a test of one’s endurance, faith, and resilience. Trekking at high altitudes, with elevations exceeding 5,000 meters, poses significant challenges, including altitude sickness, extreme weather conditions, and rugged terrain. However, with proper preparation, including physical fitness training, acclimatization, and mental preparedness, pilgrims can overcome these obstacles and experience the spiritual rewards that await them.

Best Time for Kailash Mansarovar Yatra

Choosing the right time to undertake the Kailash Mansarovar Yatra is crucial for a safe and rewarding pilgrimage. The optimal time is from May to October, when the weather is relatively mild, with clear skies and comfortable temperatures. However, pilgrims should avoid the monsoon season (July to September) and the harsh winter months (November to April), which present significant challenges for travel and trekking.

Weather Condition

The weather conditions around Mount Kailash can be unpredictable and harsh, with rapid changes in temperature and occasional snowfall. While daytime temperatures may range from 0°C to 33°C, nighttime temperatures can plummet below freezing, especially at higher altitudes. Pilgrims should be prepared for varying weather conditions by packing appropriate clothing, including layers for insulation, waterproof outerwear, and sturdy footwear.
