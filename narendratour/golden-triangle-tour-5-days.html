Tour Overview

Our Indian culture is filled with varieties of history, which sounds interesting. See More

People from all over the world travel and learn about our history. We should be proud and always take the chance to explore our culture. Now, discover one of the outstanding destinations recommended by Namaste India trip which is a Golden Triangle Tour for 5 days package with Gaya Bihar Bihar, Agra, and Jaipur.

You will definitely enjoy and feel satisfied. It also can be beneficial for students to learn faster about our Indian history where you will see Old and New Gaya Bihar Bihar along with Red fort, Humayun’s tomb, Connaught place, and many other attractions.

Agra, which is termed as UNESCO world heritage, is famous for its complex mausoleum, the Taj Mahal known for its Mughal architecture. Many tourists visit Agra for handmade and hand-knotted carpets crafted from silk, cotton, and wool. So, get ready to pack your bags and have an amazing experience here.

Let’s move towards Jaipur, capital of Rajasthan. Jaipur is known for its unique culture with stunning temples, forts, bazaars, and many more. Enjoy an exciting

4 nights 5 days Golden Triangle Tour which is going to be very adventurous.

Tour ItinerarySee More

Day 1: Arrival at Gaya Bihar Bihar

After reaching Gaya Bihar Bihar, our representative would be glad to pick you from the airport/railway station to your hotel. After a short rest, we will explore the best plac Gaya Bihar Gaya Bihar like Raj Ghat, Red fort, President’s house, Qutub Minar, etc. In the evening, arrive back at the hotel and enjoy a restful night.

Places You’ll See

Day 2: Gaya Bihar Bihar to Agra

After having a delicious meal in the morning, proceed to Agra. En-route you can visit Sikandra. After arriving in Agra, check in at the hotel and be ready to explore India’s best monument, the Taj Mahal. According to history records it took 22 years to build the Taj Mahal, This shows how much effort was put into building it. Therefore many tourists visit here to see its beauty as it is also a symbol of love. After this, you will visit Agra Fort and then return to the hotel for an overnight stay.

Places You’ll See

Day 3: Agra to Jaipur)

Check out of the hotel after breakfast and proceed to Jaipur. On the way, we will visit Fatehpur Sikri, a stunning example of the fusion between Hindu and Muslim architecture. Highlights include the Fatehpur Sikri Mosque, which features elements of both Persian and Hindu styles, as well as the Jodha Bai Palace and Buland Darwaza. Upon arrival in Jaipur, we will provide you with accommodation at a comfortable hotel for the night.

Places You’ll See

Day 4: A whole day trip in Jaipur City

Next day after breakfast, start the journey at Hawa Mahal, which is famous for its unique, iconic design and looks like a honeycomb, and leans at an angle of 87 degrees. A perfect place for photography. Later, Jal Mahal, Amber fort, Jantar Mantar, will be explored. Apart from this, if time allows you can also go to bazaars in the evening because textiles and jewelry are Jaipur’s major attractions. Then get back to the hotel to stay overnight.

Places You’ll See

Day 5: Departure

Once breakfast is finished, just check out of the hotel and we will transfer you for Gaya Bihar Bihar or Jaipur airport/railway based on your preference and get back home with a lot of photos as well as memories. Get in touch with us once more for the upcoming tour.

Things to Carry

  • Comfortable Footwear: Since you'll be exploring historical sites and markets, comfortable walking shoes are a must.
  • Weather-Appropriate Clothing: Pack lightweight and breathable clothing suitable for India's varying temperatures. Include layers for cooler evenings.
  • Sun Protection: Carry sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat to protect yourself from the sun, especially during outdoor sightseeing.
  • Water Bottle: Stay hydrated throughout the day by carrying a refillable water bottle.
  • Camera or Smartphone: Capture the beautiful architecture and moments during your tour.
  • Power Bank: Keep your devices charged for navigation and capturing photos throughout the day.
  • Medications and First Aid Kit: Carry any necessary medications and a basic first aid kit for emergencies.
  • Local Currency: Have some cash handy for small purchases and tips, though credit cards are widely accepted.
  • Travel Adapter: Ensure you can charge your devices with a universal travel adapter suitable for Indian electrical outlets.

Travel Tips

  • Plan Early Mornings: Start your day early to avoid crowds, especially at popular spots like the Taj Mahal.
  • Respect Local Customs: Dress modestly at religious sites and be mindful of local traditions for a smoother experience.
  • Stay Hydrated: Carry a water bottle and drink plenty of fluids, especially in India's warmer climate.
  • Shop Smart: Bargain respectfully in local markets for souvenirs and gifts to get the best prices.
  • Capture Memories: Bring a camera or smartphone to capture the stunning architecture and landscapes.
  • Keep Valuables Safe: Stay alert in crowded places and use hotel safes for important belongings.
  • Try Local Food Safely: Sample local cuisine at reputable restaurants to savor the flavors without worries.
